Improving the Trigger Function of Deep Convection in Tropical Regions With Deep Learning

Chen, M., Fu, H., Zhang, T., & Wang, L. (2023). ResU‐Deep: Improving the Trigger Function of Deep Convection in Tropical Regions With Deep Learning. Journal of Advances in Modeling Earth Systems, 15(11), e2022MS003521.

MAIN FINDINGS:

- Terrain information, temporal dependence of convection, and water vapor content are essential for predicting convection.

- Including information from neighboring atmospheric columns can improve the performance of the deep convection trigger function

BACKGROUND

Deep convection is always related with heavy precipitation or storms. The deep convection trigger function is a widely-used method to determine the occurrence of deep convection in general circulation models. However, many trigger functions face the overestimation of deep convections and cannot accurately capture the variations of occurrence frequency. Therefore, we propose a convection trigger function based on deep learning, called ResU-Deep, to improve the trigger function in tropical regions.

DATA

The European Center for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts (ECMWF) fifth generation atmospheric reanalysis (ERA5) data and the TRMM 3B42 data from NASA are used to construct the trigger function of deep convection.

METHODS

In this study, we adopt U-net to construct the ResU-Deep convection trigger function with three modifications:

  1. We use a convolution plus a residual block to replace the double convolution block to improve the training efficiency with huge volumes of data;
  2. We add upsampling blocks to the encoder part to fuse different scales of features during learning the mecha- nism of convection
  3. We adopt a location-aware weight to the loss function to tackle the imbalanced class.
